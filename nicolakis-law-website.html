About the Firm

IP Counsel Built on Decades of Experience

The Law Offices of Nikitas E. Nicolakis is a boutique intellectual property practice serving clients from emerging startups to established public corporations. The firm was founded on the principle that clients deserve sophisticated, high-caliber legal counsel delivered with personal attention and responsiveness.


With deep expertise in patent prosecution — both domestic and international — as well as infringement and validity opinions, the firm provides strategic IP advice that aligns with each client's unique business objectives. Our practice also encompasses trademark law, copyright, trade secrets, and related federal and state matters.


Prior to founding the firm, Nikitas E. Nicolakis honed his craft at prominent New York and national law firms, and served as a Supervising Assistant District Attorney in Rockland County, bringing a prosecutor's instinct for strategy and advocacy to every matter.

Behind the Work

N.E.N.
Nikitas E. Nicolakis
Principal · Patent Attorney
Nikitas E. Nicolakis is an experienced patent attorney with a career spanning large multinational corporations, prominent New York law firms, and the public sector. He founded the Law Offices of Nikitas E. Nicolakis to deliver sophisticated intellectual property counsel with the focused attention that only a boutique practice can provide.
Nikitas specializes in patent prosecution — both domestic and international — as well as infringement and validity opinions. His practice encompasses the full range of intellectual property law, including trademarks, copyright, trade secrets, IP licensing, antitrust, and federal and state matters. He also serves as Of Counsel at Lombard & Geliebter LLP in New York.
Prior to establishing his own practice, Nikitas served for nearly a decade as an Assistant District Attorney in Rockland County, including several years as Supervising ADA — an experience that sharpened his instincts for litigation strategy and advocacy. Earlier in his career, he held associate positions at Hamilton, Brook, Smith & Reynolds; Lowenstein Sandler; Chadbourne & Parke; and Ostrow Kaufman (formerly the Dreier LLP Patent Group), where he developed expertise in patent prosecution, licensing, and transactional IP matters.
Nikitas holds a J.D. from New York Law School, where he was a member of the Institute for Information Law & Policy, focusing on intellectual property and patent law. He also conducted student research at the Berkman Center for Internet & Society at Harvard Law School, concentrating on intellectual property, copyright, and digital media. He earned his B.S. in Computer Science from Northeastern University — a technical foundation that proves invaluable in understanding the inventions he prosecutes.
