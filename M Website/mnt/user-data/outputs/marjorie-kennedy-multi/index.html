About Marjorie

Compassionate care for every chapter of life

I am a Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist with a Master's in Social Work. I have been helping individuals and couples navigate life's most challenging moments for over 10 years, bringing warmth, evidence-based expertise, and deep respect to every therapeutic relationship.

Change, even when necessary, can feel overwhelming. Whether you're navigating the challenges of divorce, healing from trauma, overcoming anxiety, breaking free from codependent patterns, or simply seeking greater self-understanding — you don't have to go through it alone.

I believe that therapy is not one size fits all. Treatment should be tailored to meet each client's unique individual needs and goals. I provide a compassionate, affirming, and trauma-informed space where clients can learn to build stronger, more authentic relationships with themselves and others.

Healing takes courage — but you don't have to do it alone. I am here to support your journey towards greater clarity and connection.

My Philosophy

My Approach

I am a Humanistic and Bowenian-oriented therapist. I believe that rapport between therapist and client is the cornerstone of successful therapy. My treatment approach incorporates a combination of classical evidence-based practices and eastern mindfulness-based approaches.

Bowenian CBT Emotionally Focused Family Systems Humanistic Mindfulness-Based (MBCT) Relational Strength-Based
Therapy is not one size fits all. I strive to tailor every aspect of our work to your unique needs, goals, and pace. You are the expert on your own life — my role is to help you access the clarity and strength already within you.
My practice draws from Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Emotionally Focused Therapy, Family Systems, Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT), and Bowenian approaches — blending what the evidence supports with what resonates most for you.
I provide a compassionate, affirming, and trauma-informed space where every client is met with respect and without judgment. Understanding the role of past experience is central to the work we do together.
Building a genuine therapeutic relationship is the cornerstone of everything I do. During our early sessions we take time to understand the beliefs and patterns shaping your daily life, before developing a clear, collaborative plan toward your goals.
My goal is not just to help you feel better in the short term — it is to help you develop the skills, awareness, and self-compassion to sustain meaningful change long after our work together ends.
